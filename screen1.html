On March 19th, 1938, the Socialist Appeal, the Official Weekly Organ of the Socialist Workers Personality published the phrase, Lenin's General Staff of 1917 - Stalin the Executioner Alone Remains. While this phrase is used as socialist critique, this expression portrays Joseph Stalin's rise to power. Stalin, the dominant and concentrating power in the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, ruled with an Iron Fist and eliminated any possible opponents through removing, marginalizing, or executing. Under the photo of each member of the Bolshevik Central Committee of 1917, is the fate under Stalin's reign of terror.

Preceding Joseph Stalin, Nikita Khrushchev stunned the communist world with his “Secret Speech,” titled “On the Cult of Personality and Its Consequences.” Of the many criticisms, Khrushchev's disdain for Stalin's harsh ruling highlighted the failure of the mass purges and usage of repression and terror as a method of control. Khrushchev's speech pushed new measures of relaxing repression and censorship that undermined every corner of Soviet life with de-Stalinization. Entering the Cold War, Khrushchev believed that the Soviet Union and the USA's opposing ideologies could coexist peacefully. Rather than racing towards armament, Khrushchev insisted on racing towards space or the greater standard of living.
